The Count of Monte Cristo quotes

"There is neither happiness nor misery in the world; there is only the comparison of one state with another, nothing more.
He who has felt the deepest grief is best able to experience supreme happiness."

"Until the day when God will deign to reveal the future to man,
all human wisdom is contained in these two words: 'Wait and hope."

"Life is a storm. One minute you will bathe under the sun and the next you will be shattered upon the rocks.
That's when you shout, 'Do your worst, for I will do mine!' and you will be remembered forever."
